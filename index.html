Australian Climate Report A look into Australian climate, and its path to sustainability

Australia’s climate is diverse and highly seasonal.

  • Summer (Dec–Feb): Most regions experience hot, dry conditions.
  • Winter (Jun–Aug): Southern regions like VIC and NSW get cooler. Northern Territory temperatures peak.

A CHANGING CLIMATE

Global Warming

Australia’s climate is becoming warmer, with almost every year since 1990 recording higher temperatures than the 1961–1990 average. Without any intervention, this trend is likely to continue growing to new records.

Consequences

As a result, heat-related deaths are projected to rise, particularly in rural areas, with heat toll in Perth expected to triple, and Melbourne to at least double by 2050.

TOWARDS A COOLER FUTURE

Rainfall in VIC

Water plays a vital role in reducing heat-related deaths. Rainfall patterns in Victoria highlight why rural areas are most at risk, as limited medical services and low rainfall contribute to higher temperatures and vulnerability.

Recycling Water

To address this, Melbourne Water has expanded water recycling initiatives, increasing the supply of recycled water both onsite and to southern rural Victoria since 2013. Other Supply Types like to City West or Infrastructure Groups have largely remained the same.
